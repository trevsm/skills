# Objection: Hiring Pool

## Objection (Steelmanned)

Rust may be technically attractive, but the hiring pool is smaller than TypeScript, Java, or Go. A default language that makes every new team harder to staff can damage delivery capacity even if individual services are more robust.

## Pass Criteria

The reply MUST:

- Treat hiring as a first-class adoption cost.
- Explain why the scoped default remains rational despite a smaller hiring pool.

The reply MUST NOT:

- Pretend Rust hiring is as easy as TypeScript hiring.
- Move from "default" to "rare exception" without updating the thesis.

## Known Weak Replies

- "Good engineers can learn anything."
- "Rust developers are more passionate."

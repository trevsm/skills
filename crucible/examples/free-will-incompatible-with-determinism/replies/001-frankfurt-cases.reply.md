# Reply: Frankfurt Cases

## Reply

Frankfurt cases weaken the alternative-possibilities requirement, but the thesis targets desert-responsibility through ultimate sourcehood. In the case, the agent may act from their own reasons, but under determinism those reasons and the agent's character still trace to prior states outside the agent's ultimate control. The case shows that "could have done otherwise" is not the only issue; it does not show that deterministic sourcehood is enough for basic desert.

## Commitments This Reply Locks In

- The suite tests free will as desert-responsibility, not voluntary action.
- Frankfurt cases challenge alternative possibilities but not ultimate sourcehood.

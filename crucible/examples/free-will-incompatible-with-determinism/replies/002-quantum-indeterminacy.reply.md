# Reply: Quantum Indeterminacy

## Reply

The thesis is conditional: if determinism is true, desert-responsibility fails. Quantum indeterminacy may challenge the antecedent, but it does not by itself supply ultimate responsibility. Randomness can prevent an action from being fixed by prior causes, but a random element is not controlled by the agent in the basic-desert sense. That may support a different libertarian thesis, but it does not defeat the conditional incompatibility claim.

## Commitments This Reply Locks In

- Randomness does not create desert-responsibility.
- The thesis does not claim determinism is actually true.

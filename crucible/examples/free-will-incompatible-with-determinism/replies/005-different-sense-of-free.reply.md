# Reply: Different Sense Of Free

## Reply

Compatibilist freedom preserves something important: reasons-responsive, uncoerced action. The thesis does not deny that concept. It targets desert-responsibility because many praise, blame, guilt, and punishment practices appeal to more than usefulness or voluntary action; they appeal to what agents basically deserve. If compatibilism offers a replacement concept, that may be valuable, but replacement is not compatibility with the original desert-responsibility target.

## Commitments This Reply Locks In

- Compatibilist freedom may preserve reasons-responsive, uncoerced action.
- Replacement of desert-responsibility is not preservation of desert-responsibility.

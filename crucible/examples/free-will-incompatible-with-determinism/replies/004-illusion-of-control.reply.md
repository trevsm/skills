# Reply: Illusion Of Control

## Reply

The thesis does not deny that responsibility practices can be useful or that deliberation changes behavior. It denies desert-responsibility: the claim that agents ultimately deserve praise or blame independent of prior causes. A deterministic society can still justify sanctions, praise, and rehabilitation by forward-looking effects; those practices do not require the stronger metaphysical claim that people are ultimate sources of themselves.

## Commitments This Reply Locks In

- Useful responsibility practices can survive even if desert-responsibility fails.
- Forward-looking justification is distinct from basic desert.

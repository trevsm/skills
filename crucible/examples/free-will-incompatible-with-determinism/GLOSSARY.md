# Glossary

**Determinism**: The view that every event is fixed by prior states of the world plus laws of nature.

**Free will**: In this suite, the power needed for desert-responsibility: being ultimately responsible in a way that makes praise or blame deserved independently of prior causes.
_Avoid_: voluntary action, lack of coercion.

**Desert-responsibility**: Responsibility in the basic-desert sense: an agent deserves praise or blame in a way not reducible to social utility, deterrence, or rehabilitation.

**Compatibilism**: The view that free will is compatible with determinism, often by analyzing freedom as acting according to one's reasons, desires, or higher-order endorsements.

**Ultimate responsibility**: Sourcehood strong enough that responsibility does not bottom out in factors outside the agent's control.

**Agent causation**: A libertarian proposal that agents can initiate actions without being wholly determined by prior events.

**Frankfurt case**: A thought experiment intended to show that moral responsibility does not require alternative possibilities.

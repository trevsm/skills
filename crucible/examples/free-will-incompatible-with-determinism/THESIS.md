# Thesis: Free Will Is Incompatible With Determinism

## Claim

If determinism is true, then agents do not have free will in the desert-responsibility sense: they are not ultimately responsible in the way required to deserve praise or blame independent of prior causes.

## Invariants

- The thesis targets desert-responsibility, not everyday voluntary action.
- The thesis does not deny that deliberation, reasons, or social practices affect behavior.
- The thesis assumes determinism means each event is fixed by prior states plus laws.
- The thesis allows that compatibilist "freedom" may describe something useful but denies that it preserves desert-responsibility.

## What Would Defeat This

- A coherent account of ultimate responsibility compatible with deterministic causal closure.
- An argument showing that desert-responsibility does not require alternative origination or control over the source of action.
- A reason to treat compatibilist freedom as the same target concept rather than a useful replacement.

## Out Of Scope

- Legal responsibility as a social institution.
- Whether determinism is actually true.
- Whether quantum indeterminacy exists.

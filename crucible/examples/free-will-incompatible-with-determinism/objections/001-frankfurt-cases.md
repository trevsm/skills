# Objection: Frankfurt Cases

## Objection (Steelmanned)

Frankfurt cases show that responsibility does not require alternative possibilities. If an agent acts from their own reasons, and a counterfactual intervener would only have forced the same act if needed, the agent seems responsible even though they could not have done otherwise. Determinism may remove alternatives without removing responsibility.

## Pass Criteria

The reply MUST:

- Distinguish alternative possibilities from ultimate sourcehood.
- Preserve the thesis's focus on desert-responsibility.

The reply MUST NOT:

- Merely deny the intuition of Frankfurt cases.
- Shift to legal or social responsibility.

## Known Weak Replies

- "The agent could have done otherwise somehow."
- "Frankfurt cases are unrealistic."
